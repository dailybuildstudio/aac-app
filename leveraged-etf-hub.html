3 The Evidence & Frameworks

Five rules that survive contact with the data.

Daily reset = path dependency

Leveraged ETFs deliver a multiple of the daily return only. Over longer windows, returns depend on the path, not just start-to-end. Smooth trend → leverage helps; chop → it bleeds.

Volatility decay is structural

A flat-but-choppy index produces a guaranteed loss in a 3x product (see Section 1). It's geometry, not fees. This is why SOXL has run multiple reverse splits over its life.

Designed for days, not years

Direxion explicitly states these seek daily objectives and are for short holding periods with active monitoring. Every extra day held is a fresh bet the trend stays smooth.

Drawdowns are brutal & asymmetric

At 3x, a ~33% single-day index drop is near-total loss. SOXL fell ~80%+ peak-to-trough in 2022. A 80% loss needs a 400% gain to recover. Survival > maximizing any single trade.

The behavior gap, amplified

Investors chronically buy high / sell low, losing ~1–3%/yr to themselves (DALBAR, Morningstar "Mind the Gap"). Leverage multiplies that self-inflicted gap. A written plan is the antidote.

Regime & size are the real levers

What you can control: when (trend up, vol contained), how much (risk capital, small %), and your exit (defined in advance). You cannot control the market — only your exposure to it.
