# productconsulting.ai — Business Plan

*Last updated: 2026-05-27*

---

## 1. One-line thesis

**Product thinking for AI products.** We help companies turn AI ambition into AI features that actually ship and get used — applying real product-management rigor to the work, not strategy decks.

The wedge: most AI consultancies are engineering-led and weak on product judgment; most product consultants aren't AI-native. productconsulting.ai owns the intersection, and the literal `.ai` domain reinforces it instantly.

---

## 2. Why now (the market)

- **AI consulting is growing ~26%/yr** — roughly $11B (2025) to a projected $90B+ by 2035.
- **95% of enterprise GenAI pilots deliver no measurable P&L impact** (MIT, 2025). The failure is product/integration, not model quality. *That gap is the entire business.*
- **~67% of organizations know they need AI but have no strategy.** Bought/partnered AI succeeds ~2x more often than internal builds — a direct argument to hire an outside product leader.
- **The risk:** generic "AI consultant" is commoditizing fast and is increasingly automatable. Specialists command **30–40% fee premiums**. Niching is not optional — it's the moat.

**Verdict:** Strong demand *if* positioned as a specialist boutique with a sharp promise. A generic AI-consulting play is moderate-to-weak.

---

## 3. Target customer (the niche)

**Primary: Seed to Series A founders/CEOs adding AI to an existing product.**

- Too small for McKinsey/Accenture/BCG (engagements start at $500K+).
- Underserved by AI engineering boutiques, who can build but can't make product calls.
- Have budget, urgency, and a real product to improve — not a science project.
- Decision-maker is one person (founder/CEO), so sales cycles are short.

**Secondary / expansion: one regulated or "boring" vertical** — healthtech, fintech, legaltech, or vertical SaaS for trades. High willingness to pay, low competition, and domain depth compounds your reputation. Pick this as the focus *after* the first 3–5 engagements reveal where you have the strongest results and network.

**Who we explicitly do NOT serve (at launch):** enterprises wanting org-wide AI transformation, pure R&D/model-training work, and "build me an app from scratch" with no existing product.

## 4. The offer ladder

Start with one low-risk, easy-to-budget offer, then climb to recurring and high-ticket revenue. Each rung naturally sells the next.

| # | Offer | Price (USD) | What it is | Why it works |
|---|-------|-------------|------------|--------------|
| 1 | **AI Product Audit** | $3,000 flat (2 wks) | Fixed-scope review of their product + AI opportunities, ranked by ROI/effort, with a concrete roadmap. | The on-ramp. Easy to say yes to, no audience needed, becomes the lead magnet for everything else. |
| 2 | **AI Product Sprint** | $12,000–$18,000 (4–6 wks) | Hands-on: define + spec + de-risk one high-value AI feature, working with their team to a shippable plan or prototype. | Turns the audit's #1 recommendation into action. Tangible outcome → referenceable case study. |
| 3 | **Advisory retainer** | $4,000–$8,000/mo | Ongoing product guidance: roadmap, prioritization, reviews, "AI judgment on call." | Recurring revenue, light delivery load. Natural follow-on: "you found it, now keep us on track." |
| 4 | **Fractional Head of AI Product** | $12,000–$20,000/mo (2–3 days/wk) | Embedded senior product leadership owning the AI product strategy and execution. | Highest income per client. Your senior-product-leader background makes this credible and defensible. Cap at 2–3 clients. |

**Pricing notes:**
- The $3K audit is deliberately a no-brainer relative to the value and to a $12K+/mo fractional engagement. It's a sales tool as much as a product.
- Anchor the audit by quoting it next to "the typical 6-month enterprise AI strategy engagement starts at $500K."
- Raise the audit to $4–5K once you have 3 case studies and a waitlist.

**Deferred (build later, once you have audience + reputation):** cohort course / workshops for product teams ($800–$2,500/seat), a paid newsletter/community, and productized playbooks/templates. These are leverage plays that need an audience the audit funnel will build — not day-one income.

## 5. Positioning & messaging

- **Headline promise:** "AI features that ship and get used — not another strategy deck."
- **Proof points to develop:** your senior product track record, before/after metrics from each engagement, named logos (with permission), a signature framework (e.g., the "AI Product Audit" scoring model).
- **Differentiator narrative:** *ownership transfer, not dependency.* A senior practitioner owns the engagement end-to-end; the client's team is more capable when you leave.
- **Tone:** sharp, senior, outcome-first. No hype, no jargon. You are the anti-"AI transformation theater" option.

---

## 6. Go-to-market

**Channels, in priority order for a solo senior operator:**

1. **Warm network first.** Your existing product/startup relationships are the fastest path to the first 3 audits. Direct, personal outreach — not a launch announcement.
2. **Founder communities** where seed/Series A CEOs gather (relevant Slack/Discord groups, accelerator alumni networks, On Deck, local startup scenes).
3. **Content as proof, not volume.** One sharp LinkedIn post or short essay per week dissecting *why a real AI feature failed or succeeded* (anonymized). This demonstrates judgment — the thing you're selling.
4. **Referrals engineered into delivery.** End every engagement by asking for one intro to a founder with the same problem.
5. **The audit as a wedge.** Offer a free 30-minute "AI opportunity teardown" call that converts to the paid audit.

**Sales motion:** Free teardown call → $3K audit → sprint or retainer → fractional. Most revenue comes from climbing the ladder with existing clients, not constant new logos.

## 7. 90-day launch plan

**Days 1–30 — Foundation & first conversations**
- Lock positioning and write the AI Product Audit scope/deliverable template.
- Ship the landing page (done — see `/website`) with a "book a teardown call" CTA.
- List 30 warm-network targets; send 10 personal outreach messages/week.
- Publish 4 LinkedIn posts (one/week) establishing the POV.
- **Goal:** 5 teardown calls booked, 1–2 audits sold.

**Days 31–60 — Deliver & prove**
- Run the first 1–2 audits; over-deliver and capture a written result + testimonial.
- Convert at least one audit into a sprint or retainer.
- Turn the first audit into an anonymized case-study post.
- **Goal:** 3 audits delivered, 1 recurring engagement (retainer or sprint) signed.

**Days 61–90 — Tighten the funnel & raise prices**
- Productize the audit further (templated deliverable, scoring model = your IP).
- Decide the secondary vertical based on where results/network are strongest.
- Pursue the first fractional Head of AI Product conversation.
- Raise audit price to $4K once a waitlist exists.
- **Goal:** $15–25K booked/recurring run-rate; pipeline for one fractional client.

## 8. Demand-validation playbook (do this before over-investing)

You don't need to guess — test the offer cheaply first:

1. **The 10-message test.** Message 10 founders in your network describing the audit. If 3+ want a call, demand is real. If 0–1, the offer or niche is wrong — adjust.
2. **The landing-page test.** Drive ~100 targeted visitors (network + 1–2 community posts) to the page. Track "book a call" clicks. >3–5% is a healthy signal.
3. **The price test.** On teardown calls, quote $3K and watch the reaction. Easy yeses → you're underpriced. Consistent flinches → wrong buyer or unclear value, not necessarily wrong price.
4. **Kill/scale rule.** If 30 days of outreach yields zero paid audits, change the *niche* before changing the *price*. The niche is the most common point of failure.

## 10. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Commoditization of "AI consulting" | Niche hard (stage + vertical); sell product *judgment*, which doesn't commoditize. |
| Time cap (solo, fractional caps at 2–3 clients) | Use audits/sprints to stay leveraged; add course/digital products once audience exists. |
| Credibility cold-start | Lead with your track record + over-deliver on first 2 audits to manufacture proof fast. |
| AI itself automating the work | Position above the automatable layer: strategy, prioritization, org/product judgment. |
| Feast-or-famine pipeline | Engineer referrals into every engagement; keep a steady 1-post/week content cadence. |
