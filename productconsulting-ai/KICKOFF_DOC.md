# Client Kickoff Doc — AI Product Audit

> Send this within 24 hours of contract signature. Sets expectations, collects what you need, and signals "this is a real engagement, not a vibes call."

---

## Subject line for the email
**Welcome to your AI Product Audit — quick logistics**

## The email body

> Hi [CLIENT NAME],
>
> Excited to get into it. The audit officially kicks off [START DATE]; final report lands by [END DATE].
>
> A few things to set us up well — none of these should take more than 30 minutes total from your side.
>
> **1) Pre-call: send me a few things by [START DATE - 2 days]**
> - Read-only product login (or a 5-minute Loom walkthrough if your product needs context)
> - Your current AI feature ideas, in any form (Notion, doc, bullet list)
> - The 1–2 metrics you most want to move with AI (activation, retention, revenue, etc.)
> - Any prior internal AI strategy work — even half-finished
>
> **2) Stakeholder interviews — 60 min each, up to 3 people**
> Designate the 2–3 people I should talk to. Best mix: a product lead, an engineer who'd build the AI work, and either a customer-facing person (CS / support) or a founder. I'll send Cal.com links for scheduling.
>
> **3) Kickoff call — 45 minutes, [PROPOSED DATE/TIME]**
> Just you and me. Cover the audit scope, confirm what you've sent, align on what success looks like.
>
> The full timeline is in your SOW. Two things to know:
> - Day 9–11: I send you a draft report. I need your feedback within 3 business days for the timeline to hold.
> - Day 14: final report + walkthrough call.
>
> Anything you want to flag now? Hit reply.
>
> — Amanda

## What to attach to the email
- The signed SOW (PDF)
- The signed MSA (PDF)
- Invoice for the 50% deposit (if not already sent)
- This kickoff doc (or a Notion/Google Doc version with the same content)

## Internal checklist — DO before kickoff call

- [ ] Confirm deposit has cleared (Stripe / bank account)
- [ ] Add client to your CRM / pipeline tracker
- [ ] Create a folder: `clients/[CLIENT_NAME]/` with subfolders `materials/`, `interviews/`, `drafts/`, `final/`
- [ ] Block 20% buffer time in your calendar across the 2-week window
- [ ] Pre-read everything they sent
- [ ] Prepare 3–5 thoughtful questions about their product, written down

## During kickoff call — 45-min structure

1. **(5 min) Welcome + confirm logistics.** Both names spelled right, timezones, primary point of contact, communication channel preference (email vs Slack vs Notion comments).
2. **(10 min) Their product, in their words.** Let them walk you through it. Listen for tension points and metric language.
3. **(15 min) What's prompted the AI question now?** Pressure-test. Is it a board ask, a competitive response, a real user need, or a "we should be doing this" panic? The honest answer changes the audit's framing.
4. **(10 min) Confirm scope and success criteria.** What does a "great audit" look like to them? Get this in writing.
5. **(5 min) Next steps.** Confirm interview slots, draft delivery date, walkthrough date. End on time.

## Within 1 hour after kickoff call
- Send a 1-paragraph recap email: what we agreed on, when next deliverables hit, what's blocking you (if anything)
- Update their record in your tracker
- Add any new info to the audit folder

A short email that prevents surprises:

> Hi [NAME],
>
> Halfway point. Quick update:
>
> - Interviews complete: [LIST]
> - Top 3 opportunity candidates so far: [BRIEF LIST, no scoring yet]
> - One open question I'd love your take on: [SPECIFIC QUESTION]
>
> Draft report still on track for [DRAFT DATE]. Anything to flag?
>
> — Amanda

This single email separates senior consultants from gigs. Clients want to know they're not being ghosted between contract and delivery.
