# Launch Checklist — What You're Missing

*The business plan and website cover positioning and demand. This covers the unglamorous operational, legal, and financial scaffolding that quietly sinks solo consultants. Prioritized so you don't over-build before you have a client.*

> ⚠️ **Not legal or tax advice.** Entity formation, contracts, and taxes vary by jurisdiction — confirm specifics with a lawyer and an accountant. The point here is to make sure nothing important is *missing from your radar*.

---

## Tier 1 — Before you take a single dollar
*The minimum to operate professionally and not expose yourself to risk. Most of this is a few hours total.*

### Legal & money basics
- [ ] **Business entity.** An LLC (or local equivalent) separates personal assets from business liability and looks credible on contracts. Sole proprietorship is simpler but offers no liability shield. *Decide with an accountant — it also affects taxes.*
- [ ] **Business bank account.** Never commingle personal and business money. Opens cleanly once the entity exists.
- [ ] **A contract you can send.** You need two documents (often combined):
  - **Master Services Agreement (MSA)** — the legal terms (liability cap, IP, confidentiality, payment terms, termination).
  - **Statement of Work (SOW)** — what you'll deliver, when, for how much, for *this* engagement.
  - *Don't write these from scratch. Start from a vetted template (e.g., from a service like Bonsai, or a lawyer-reviewed one) and have a lawyer review once.*
- [ ] **IP & confidentiality terms.** Be explicit: the client owns the deliverables; you may keep anonymized learnings; both sides keep secrets confidential. Critical when you'll see their product internals.
- [ ] **Payment terms that protect you.** Standard for this model: **50% deposit upfront, 50% on delivery** for the audit; retainers billed monthly in advance. State a late fee and that work pauses on non-payment.

### Getting paid
- [ ] **Invoicing + payments.** Stripe, or an invoicing tool (Wave is free, QuickBooks/FreshBooks if you want bookkeeping too). Make it trivially easy to pay you.
- [ ] **Bookkeeping habit.** Even a spreadsheet to start. Track income, expenses, and set aside ~25–30% for taxes from day one. Talk to an accountant before your first big month.

### The actual operation
- [ ] **Scheduling link.** Cal.com (free) or Calendly. **Wire this into the website's "Book a teardown call" CTA** — it currently points to a mailto placeholder (`TODO` in `index.html`).
- [ ] **Professional email on the domain.** `hello@productconsulting.ai` / `you@productconsulting.ai` via Google Workspace or Fastmail. Sending from a gmail address undercuts a premium offer.
- [ ] **The audit deliverable is ready to fulfill.** `AUDIT_TEMPLATE.md` is your starting point — make sure you can actually deliver it before you sell it.

## Tier 2 — Before / as you scale past the first client
*Add these once money is coming in. Don't front-load them.*

- [ ] **Professional liability insurance (E&O).** Errors & omissions coverage protects you if a client claims your advice caused a loss. Cheap relative to the downside; some clients require it.
- [ ] **A simple CRM / pipeline.** A Notion board or Airtable is plenty. Track: lead → call booked → audit → upsell. (The tracking table in `OUTREACH.md` is enough to start.)
- [ ] **Proposal + onboarding templates.** A repeatable proposal (so you're not rewriting each time) and a kickoff doc (what you need from the client, timeline, point of contact).
- [ ] **Site analytics.** Plausible/Fathom (privacy-friendly) or GA. *Placeholder is already in `index.html` — just paste the snippet.* You can't run the demand-validation tests in the plan without it.
- [ ] **Email capture, not just a call link.** Most visitors aren't ready to book. Offer a lead magnet (e.g., a one-page "AI Product Audit checklist" PDF) in exchange for an email, so you can follow up. Use a simple form (ConvertKit, Buttondown, or a Tally form).
- [ ] **Testimonial + case-study process.** Bake the ask into delivery (script is in `OUTREACH.md`). Get permission in writing to use names/logos. **This is the single highest-impact upgrade to the website** — replace the placeholder quotes ASAP.
- [ ] **Privacy policy / basic legal pages** on the site if you start collecting emails (and to be safe with EU/CA visitors).

## Blind spots specific to *this* business
*Things easy to miss in a solo, senior-advisory model:*

1. **Utilization math.** Fractional work caps at 2–3 clients. Map how many of each engagement type it takes to hit your income target — and don't oversell your calendar. (See the economics table in the business plan.)
2. **Client concentration risk.** If one fractional client is 60% of revenue, their budget cut is your crisis. Keep a pipeline even when "full."
3. **Conflict of interest.** Two clients in the same niche can create real or perceived conflicts. Decide your policy (and put a clause in the MSA) before it happens.
4. **Confidentiality vs. marketing.** You'll want case studies; clients will want discretion. Default to anonymized results and get explicit written sign-off for anything named.
5. **The credibility cold-start.** With no testimonials yet, your *own* track record and an over-delivered first audit are the proof. Manufacture proof fast — consider a discounted or free first audit for one ideal-fit client purely to generate a case study.
6. **Scope creep.** Your biggest margin killer. The fixed-scope audit is your defense — hold the line, and route "can you also..." into the next paid engagement.
7. **Niche commitment.** The research is blunt: generalists get commoditized, specialists earn 30–40% more. The instinct under early pressure is to say yes to everything. Resist it — niche down, don't broaden.

## The honest critical path (if you do nothing else)
1. Entity + bank account + a reviewed contract template.
2. Scheduling link wired into the site + domain email.
3. Confirm you can deliver the audit (`AUDIT_TEMPLATE.md`).
4. Send 10 outreach messages (`OUTREACH.md`).
5. Sell one audit, over-deliver, get a testimonial + a referral.

Everything else can wait until that loop is working.
