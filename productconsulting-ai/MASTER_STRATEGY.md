## The Big Idea

**productconsulting.ai is not a generic AI consultancy. It is the product judgment layer that fintech and financial-services teams are missing.**

The AI consulting market is flooded with engineers who can call an API and strategists who can write a slide deck. Neither group can do what a senior product leader does: decide *what* to build, scope it so it actually ships, and measure whether it moved the metric. That gap — between AI ambition and shipped AI product — is the business.

Primary niche: **fintech and financial-services product teams** adding AI to regulated, high-stakes products.
Secondary niche: retail tech and adtech product teams (warm network, secondary outreach).

## The Core Problem to Solve

AI features fail not because of bad models — because of bad product decisions around them.

| Failure mode | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| **Wrong bets** | Features chosen by hype or demo appeal, not by ROI and user value. Engineering effort goes to things nobody needed. |
| **Scope that never ships** | "AI everywhere" roadmaps that balloon, lose momentum, and stall before reaching users. |
| **Built but unused** | Features that launch to a press release and flatline — no adoption, no measurable impact. |
| **Compliance kills** | In fintech: perfectly good AI features that die in risk review because no one thought about the regulatory angle during product design. |

The result: product and engineering teams waste 6–18 months building AI features that don't move the business. External product judgment — someone who's done this before and has no internal political agenda — cuts through it faster than any internal process.

## Positioning

*Product thinking for AI products — specifically for fintech and financial-services teams who need to ship AI features that move the business and survive compliance.*

- Can evaluate whether a proposed AI feature is worth building before engineering starts

## The Offer Ladder

Designed as a low-friction on-ramp with natural upsell to recurring revenue.

| Step | Offer | Price | Duration | What they get |
|------|-------|-------|----------|---------------|
| **0 · Free** | AI Product Teardown Call | $0 | 30 min | Pressure-test one AI idea live. They leave with a concrete next step. You leave with a qualified lead or a no. |

| **3 · Recurring** | Advisory Retainer | $4K–$8K/mo | Ongoing | Roadmap reviews, prioritization, product judgment on-call as they keep shipping. |
| **4 · Embedded** | Fractional Head of AI Product | $12K–$20K/mo | 2–3 days/week | Senior product leadership owning AI strategy and execution end-to-end. Limited to 2–3 clients at a time. |

- Month 1–2: 2 audits = $6,000
- Month 3–4: 1 audit + 1 retainer = $7,000–$11,000/mo
- Month 5–8: 1 retainer + 1 fractional = $16,000–$28,000/mo
- Steady state (2 fractional + 1 retainer): $28,000–$48,000/mo

Capacity ceiling at fractional: 2–3 clients maximum (the constraint is your time, not demand).

## Target Customer

### Primary: Fintech / Financial Services Product Teams

**Who:** VP/Director/Head of Product at:
- Fintech startups (Series A–C) building regulated products (lending, payments, credit, wealth)
- Regional banks and credit unions modernizing with AI
- Financial services SaaS companies (GreenSky, Curinos, similar)
- Insurance tech companies (AI underwriting, claims, fraud)

**Their problem:**
- Under pressure from leadership to "add AI" without a clear product strategy
- Internal teams strong on compliance/risk, weak on product judgment
- AI features keep dying in risk review because they weren't designed with compliance in mind
- Afraid of getting the wrong AI bet wrong — the regulatory consequences are expensive

**Why they hire outside help:**
- Internal teams too politically constrained to make hard prioritization calls
- No budget for a full-time CPO or Head of AI Product
- Need someone who's done this before, in their space, quickly

**Budget authority:** VP/Director of Product, sometimes CEO at Series A–C. $3K–$20K/mo is under most approval thresholds for outside advisory.

### Secondary: Retail Tech & Adtech Product Teams

**Who:** Product leaders at Lowe's, Home Depot, CarMax, and mid-market retail tech / adtech SaaS companies.

**Their problem:**
- AI roadmaps driven by hype (everyone has a "personalization" initiative that doesn't convert)
- Data sitting in warehouses that nobody has turned into a shipped AI feature
- Adtech companies specifically: cookie deprecation forcing AI-driven audience modeling rebuild — real product problem, real urgency

**Why secondary (not primary):**
- Current employer (Floor & Decor) creates sensitivity in the retail vertical
- Budgets are lower than fintech for external advisory
- Less acute regulatory pressure = less urgency to hire outside

## Content Strategy

### Voice (confirmed as authentic)
Direct, opinionated, evidence-backed. Concrete numbers over vague claims. "What NOT to do" sections. Senior operator framing ("in paid client audits") not pundit framing ("best practices say"). Short paragraphs, sharp headlines.

### Content calendar (sustainable for solo operator)
| Frequency | Channel | Asset |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Daily-light | LinkedIn comments | Engage on 3–5 posts in your network — no writing burden |
| Weekly | LinkedIn post | One sharp opinion or teardown (~200 words) |
| Monthly | Framework piece | One deeper piece or public teardown — evergreen, becomes ad creative |

### Lead magnet
**AI Product Audit Scoring Framework** (`framework.html`) — 5-dimension scoring model (User Value, Business Impact, Feasibility, Time to Value, Defensibility). Visitors self-score their AI ideas. Ends with CTA to book teardown for the real version.
