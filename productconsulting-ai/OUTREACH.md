# Outreach Scripts — productconsulting.ai

*Goal: book 5 teardown calls and sell 1–2 audits in your first 30 days. Warm network first — it's the fastest path to revenue and the only one that works without an audience.*

**Rules that make these work:**
- Personalize the first line every time. Generic = ignored.
- Lead with *them*, not you. One clear, small ask.
- Don't pitch the audit in message #1 — offer value (the teardown), let the audit come up on the call.
- Send 10/week. Track replies. Iterate on whichever version lands.

---

## A. Warm network — the direct ask
*For people who know you and know you've led product.*

> Hey [Name] — I'm starting something new: helping seed/Series A teams figure out where AI actually fits in their product (and where it doesn't). Basically the product-judgment layer most "AI consultants" skip.
>
> You came to mind because [specific reason — they're building X / mentioned AI / lead product at Y]. Not pitching you — genuinely curious whether this is a real problem for you right now. Worth a 20-min call?

---

## B. Warm network — the soft intro / referral ask
*For people who aren't the buyer but know buyers.*

> Hey [Name] — quick one. I've launched productconsulting.ai: I help founders adding AI to their product make the right build decisions and actually ship. I'm looking to talk to seed–Series A teams wrestling with "where do we use AI."
>
> Anyone in your network come to mind who's in that spot? Happy to give them a free teardown of their AI ideas — no strings. Even one intro would mean a lot.

---

## C. The teardown offer (value-first)
*Best opener for warm-ish contacts who have a product.*

> Hi [Name] — I've been heads-down on [their product] and had a couple of ideas about where AI could move [metric/outcome] for you. I just started a product-consulting practice focused exactly on this.
>
> Want me to walk you through them on a free 30-min call? Worst case you get a couple of useful ideas; no pitch unless you ask for one.

---

## D. Second-degree / cold-warm (referred or community)
*When someone was referred or you share a community.*

> Hi [Name] — [mutual] suggested I reach out / saw your post in [community] about [topic]. I help seed–Series A teams turn "we should use AI" into features that actually ship and get used — the product side, not just the engineering.
>
> I do a free 30-min teardown where we pressure-test your AI ideas live. Open to one? Here's my calendar: [link]

---

## Follow-ups
*~80% of replies come from the follow-up. Space them 3–4 days apart. Max 2.*

**Follow-up 1:**
> Hey [Name] — floating this back up in case it got buried. Still happy to do a quick teardown if useful. No worries if the timing's off.

**Follow-up 2 (the breakup):**
> Totally get it if this isn't a now thing, [Name] — I'll stop nudging. If "where does AI fit" becomes a live question down the road, my door's open. And if someone in your circle is wrestling with it, an intro is always welcome.

---

## E. Community / LinkedIn post (proof-of-judgment content)
*Post ~1x/week. Demonstrate the thinking you sell. This is marketing as proof, not volume.*

> **Most AI features fail for a boring reason: nobody decided if anyone wanted them.**
>
> I keep seeing the same pattern at seed/Series A: a team bolts an "AI assistant" onto the product because the demo looked great. Three months later — built, shipped, unused.
>
> The fix isn't a better model. It's three product questions *before* a line of code:
> 1. What user moment does this actually improve?
> 2. What metric should move — and can we measure it?
> 3. What's the smallest version that proves it?
>
> [Anonymized example: a team that almost built X, asked these, built Y instead, and saw Z.]
>
> That gap — between AI ambition and AI that ships and gets used — is the whole reason I started productconsulting.ai. If you're staring at an AI roadmap and not sure which bet is real, that's exactly the conversation I love. DMs open.

*Variations to rotate: a teardown of a public AI feature that flopped; a "what I'd build instead" thread; a contrarian take on an overhyped AI pattern.*

---

## F. The teardown call → audit (don't wing this)
*Structure the free call so it converts without giving the audit away.*

1. **(5 min) Their world.** Product, users, what's prompting the AI question now.
2. **(15 min) Pressure-test.** React to their ideas with real product judgment. Give 1–2 genuinely useful insights — enough to prove value, not the full roadmap.
3. **(5 min) Name the gap.** "There's real opportunity here, but also a couple of expensive wrong turns. Worth doing this properly."
4. **(5 min) The offer.** "That's exactly what the AI Product Audit is — two weeks, $3K, a ranked roadmap and a clear first build. Want me to send the one-pager?"
5. **Always close with a next step**, even a soft one: send the one-pager, book the follow-up.

---

## G. Referral ask (use at the end of every engagement)
*The cheapest pipeline you'll ever build. Ask while the result is fresh.*

> [Name], really glad this landed. One ask: who's the next founder you know wrestling with the same "where does AI fit" question? I'd love an intro — I'll give them the same teardown I gave you. The best clients I get come from people like you.
