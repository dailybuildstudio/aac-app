# Targeted Outreach — Network Cluster Templates
*5 templates matched to your actual LinkedIn clusters. Each is personalized to the buyer's context, not a generic consulting pitch. Personalize the [bracketed] fields — everything else is ready to send.*

**Voice rules:** Lead with them. One ask. No pitch in message #1. The teardown is the offer, not the audit.

---

## Template 1 — Financial Services Product Leaders
*For: former JPMorgan Chase colleagues, Citi/Wells Fargo/GreenSky/Curinos product connections. These buyers have budget, face real regulatory pressure to "do AI responsibly," and are drowning in vendor pitches. Your JPMorgan background is the credibility signal that opens this door — lean on it.*

**When to use:** VP/Director/Head of Product at a bank, fintech, or financial-services SaaS you're connected to. They probably have AI pilots in flight that aren't landing.

> Hey [Name] —
>
> Hope things are good at [company]. I've been heads-down building productconsulting.ai — I help financial-services product teams cut through the AI noise and figure out what's actually worth building (and what isn't).
>
> The pattern I keep seeing: a lot of fintech teams are great at the compliance and risk side of AI, and genuinely weak on the product side — what to build, for whom, how to know if it worked. That gap is where most pilots quietly stall.
>
> Given your work on [their product/team area], I'd love to get your read on where you're seeing that play out. No agenda — 25 minutes, I'll share what I'm seeing across the space and you tell me if it matches reality at [company].
>
> Worth a call?
>
> Amanda

**Follow-up (Day 5, if no reply):**
> Hey [Name] — just bumping this in case it got buried. No pressure at all. If it's not the right time, would love to reconnect another way — happy to share the AI Product Audit Scoring Framework I put together if that'd be useful context for your team. Here: https://productconsulting.ai/framework.html

## Template 2 — Retail Tech / Home Improvement Product Leaders
*For: Lowe's, Home Depot, CarMax, or similar retail-tech product connections. NOT current Floor & Decor colleagues (sensitivity — see note below). These buyers are dealing with AI use cases like search, recommendations, and associate tools. Your retail background gives you instant context.*

**⚠️ Floor & Decor note:** Avoid messaging current F&D colleagues about your consulting work until you've checked your employment agreement and talked to HR or your manager. Former F&D colleagues who've since moved to other companies = fair game.

**When to use:** Director/VP of Product or Engineering at a major retailer or retail-tech company, ideally one you've interacted with professionally.

> Hey [Name] —
>
> I've been following what [company] is doing with [AI/personalization/search] — looks like you're actually moving fast in a space where most retailers are still in pilot mode.
>
> I launched productconsulting.ai a few months ago. I help retail and commerce product teams figure out which AI bets are worth making — the ones that move GMV or NPS, not just generate demos. Given your work on [their area], I'd love to hear what's working and what's still stuck.
>
> I offer a free 30-min "teardown call" where we pressure-test an AI idea live — no pitch, you leave with something useful whether we work together or not. Worth a conversation?
>
> Amanda

**Personalization notes for this cluster:**
- Lowe's: mention their smart home / associate AI tools push
- Home Depot: their supply chain + pro contractor AI work is public
- CarMax: their "Love Your Car Guarantee" AI personalization is well-publicized

## Template 3 — Adtech / Martech Product Leaders
*For: connections from the digital advertising / martech world — DSPs, CDPs, measurement platforms, ad-tech SaaS. These buyers are uniquely pressured: cookie deprecation and privacy changes are forcing them to rebuild core features, and AI is part almost every roadmap pitch right now. Your data analytics background (Tableau Partner) is the credibility hook here.*

**When to use:** Product or data leaders at adtech/martech companies — especially anyone working on audience targeting, attribution, or first-party data products.

> Hey [Name] —
>
> Quick note — I've launched productconsulting.ai and I'm working with product teams in the adtech/martech space specifically. The problem I keep solving: teams have real AI ambitions, but the product decisions around *how* to use the data they're sitting on are murky. Too many "AI roadmap" slides, not enough shipped features.
>
> With my background in data analytics and product, I help teams cut from "we should use AI" to a ranked, actionable list of what to build first — with the ROI math to back it.
>
> Given what you're building at [company], I'd love to hear how you're thinking about the AI layer. Happy to do a free 30-min teardown of any AI idea you're wrestling with. No strings.
>
> Amanda

## Template 4 — VC Multiplier (Eric Bahn, Hustle Fund)
*For: Eric Bahn specifically — or any seed/early-stage investor in your network. Don't ask for business directly. Ask for intros. One warm intro from a trusted VC is worth 50 cold messages.*

**What makes this different:** You're not asking him to hire you. You're making it easy for him to add value to his portfolio companies. That's the frame that makes VCs respond.

> Hey Eric —
>
> Hope you're well! I launched productconsulting.ai — I help seed and Series A founders figure out which AI features are actually worth building before they sink 6 months into the wrong one. Fixed-scope, fast, and focused on getting something shipped vs. endless strategy.
>
> I know Hustle Fund sees a lot of founders right now who are under pressure to "add AI" — curious if any of your portfolio companies are wrestling with that and might benefit from a free 30-min teardown call. Happy to offer it as a resource to anyone you think could use the outside POV.
>
> No expectation of anything in return — just thought it was worth surfacing. Would love to reconnect regardless.
>
> Amanda

**Why this works:** You're offering him something to give his portfolio companies (free teardown), not asking him to evaluate you. Investors respond to "useful to my founders" far faster than "please refer me."
