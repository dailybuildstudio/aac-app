The problem

The gap isn't the model. It's the product judgment around it.

Teams can call an API. What they struggle with is deciding what's worth building, scoping it so it ships, and making sure people actually use it. That's a product problem — and it's where most AI work quietly fails.

🎯

Wrong bets

AI features chosen by hype or demo appeal, not by ROI and user value. Effort goes to things nobody needed.

🌀

Scope that never ships

Ambitious "AI everywhere" projects that balloon, lose momentum, and stall before reaching users.

📉

Built but unused

Features that launch to a press release and then flatline — no adoption, no measurable impact.

Fit

Who this is for — and who it isn't.

Sharp focus is the point. If you're outside this, I'll happily refer you to someone better suited.

✓ A great fit

  • Seed to Series A companies with a real product and real users.
  • Founders/CEOs who want AI to move a metric, not generate a headline.
  • Teams that can build but need product judgment on what to build.
  • You want a senior operator in the work, not a deck delivered from arm's length.

✕ Not a fit (yet)

FAQ

Questions, answered.

What exactly do I get from the $3,000 audit?

A written, ranked roadmap of AI opportunities for your product — each scored by ROI and effort — plus a clear recommendation on the single highest-value thing to build first, and how to de-risk it. Delivered in two weeks, with a walkthrough call.

Why start with an audit instead of just building?

Because 95% of AI pilots fail on the wrong-bet and won't-ship problems — not the engineering. Two weeks of sharp product thinking up front saves months of building something nobody uses.

Do you build the feature, or just advise?

Both are available. The audit defines what's worth building; the Sprint engagement is hands-on to spec and de-risk it with your team; ongoing retainer or fractional leadership takes it further. You choose how deep to go.

What stage and size of company is this for?

Primarily seed to Series A teams with a live product and real users. If you're earlier or much larger, I'll tell you honestly and point you to a better fit.
